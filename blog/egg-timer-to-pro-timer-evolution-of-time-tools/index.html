The Early Days of Cooking and Time Management

Cooking has been an integral part of human civilization since the dawn of time. With the discovery of fire, humans learned to cook their food, which led to the development of various cooking techniques and tools. In the early days, cooking was a time-consuming and labor-intensive process that required patience and attention to detail.

One of the earliest time management tools used in cooking was the candle clock, which was invented by the ancient Egyptians around 1500 BCE. This primitive clock used the burning of candles to measure time, with each candle representing a certain period of time. While not very accurate, the candle clock marked the beginning of time management in cooking.

From Candle Clocks to Mechanical Timers

With the invention of mechanical clocks in the Middle Ages, time management in cooking became more accurate and efficient. Mechanical timers, also known as kitchen clocks, were introduced in the 17th century and quickly became a staple in every kitchen. These timers used a series of gears and weights to measure time, allowing cooks to keep track of cooking times with greater precision.

The mechanical timer was a significant improvement over the candle clock, but it still had its limitations. It was often cumbersome, difficult to set, and required manual winding. Nevertheless, it marked an important milestone in the evolution of time management in cooking.

The Rise of Electronic Timers and Egg Timer Cooking

The advent of electronic timers in the mid-20th century revolutionized cooking and time management. Electronic timers were more accurate, easier to use, and required minimal maintenance. They also introduced the concept of egg timer cooking, which allowed cooks to precisely control the cooking time for eggs and other foods.

Egg timer cooking became a popular technique in the 1950s and 1960s, with the introduction of electronic egg timers. These timers were designed specifically for cooking eggs and other foods, with precise time settings and audible alarms. Egg timer cooking became a staple in many kitchens, and the electronic timer remained a popular time management tool for decades.
